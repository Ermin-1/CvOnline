WCAG A+AA

These 34 guidelines are intended to provide a comprehensive understanding of what is required to create and maintain an accessible website or web application according to WCAG A+AA standards.

By following these guidelines, developers can contribute to creating a more inclusive and accessible digital environment for all users.

Achived qualifications:

Structured and Semantic HTML: My code utilizes proper HTML markup and semantics to organize and structure the content of my web page in a meaningful way. This ensures that screen readers and other assistive technologies can interpret the content correctly, enhancing accessibility.

Clear and Consistent Navigation: My code employs clear and consistent navigation methods across my website, such as a navigation bar or menu, to assist users in orienting themselves. This consistency ensures that users can easily find their way around my site, improving usability.

Informative Content: Each page of my website provides clear and meaningful information, including page headings, titles, and descriptive texts. This ensures that users understand the purpose of each page and can access relevant content easily.

Alternative Text Descriptions: I provide alternative text descriptions for all non-text content elements, such as images, icons, and graphics. This ensures that users who rely on screen readers or have images disabled can understand the content conveyed by these elements.

Text Size and Contrast: Text on my website has sufficient size and contrast to be readable for all users, including those with visual impairments. This includes using appropriate color contrasts and ensuring text is large enough to be legible.

Accessible Links and Buttons: My code uses clear and descriptive link texts and button labels to enhance navigation for users. This practice improves the accessibility of my website by providing easily understandable cues for interaction.

Accessibility in Responsive Design: Your website is designed to be usable and accessible across various devices and screen sizes. This responsiveness ensures that users can access your site comfortably regardless of the device they are using.

Avoidance of Moving or Blinking Content: I refrain from using moving or blinking elements unless they can be stopped, paused, or adjusted by the user. This prevents potential distractions or discomfort for users with certain sensitivities.

Browser Compatibility: I tested my website's functionality and accessibility across different browsers to ensure full compatibility. This practice ensures that users can access your site regardless of the browser they use.

Readability and Comprehension: My code employs a simple and easily understandable language style and structure to facilitate content comprehension for all users.

Proper Use of Color: I avoid relying solely on color to convey information and ensure that information is understandable for users who may not perceive color.

Accessible Data and Tables: My use of tables and data is accessible by providing descriptive headers and structured information, making it easier for users with disabilities to understand the content.

Voidance of Solely Audio Content: I dont use audio-based content, ensuring accessibility for users with hearing impairments.

Declaration of Standard Language: My code declares the language of its content using HTML attributes to facilitate correct text interpretation, improving accessibility for users with various language skills or preferences.

Robustness and Reliability: My website is designed to be lightweight and fast-loading, ensuring compatibility across a wide range of browsers and devices. By keeping the design simple and avoiding complex features, I minimize the risk of compatibility issues or usability errors.

Use of Accessible UI Components: My website utilizes standard UI components and design patterns that are familiar to most users. By avoiding complex or custom elements, I ensure that the interface is easy to navigate and understand for everyone.
